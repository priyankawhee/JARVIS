Microbiology Notes

1. Introduction:
   - Microbiology is the study of microscopic organisms such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, and protozoa.

2. Types of Microorganisms:
   - Bacteria: Prokaryotic, single-celled organisms.
   - Viruses: Acellular entities requiring host cells to reproduce.
   - Fungi: Eukaryotic organisms; can be unicellular or multicellular.
   - Protozoa: Unicellular eukaryotes found in water and soil.

3. Applications:
   - Medicine: Production of antibiotics and vaccines.
   - Agriculture: Nitrogen fixation by bacteria.
   - Industry: Fermentation (beer, yogurt, cheese).
   - Environment: Waste decomposition and bioremediation.

4. Pathogens and Diseases:
   - Bacterial: Tuberculosis, Cholera.
   - Viral: Influenza, COVID-19.
   - Fungal: Ringworm, Candidiasis.

5. Techniques:
   - Microscopy, Culture Methods, Staining, Genetic Engineering.
